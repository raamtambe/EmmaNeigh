# EmmaNeigh Pitch Deck
## Kirkland AI Meeting
## April 6, 2026

This is the talk track behind the revised 8-slide partner-facing deck.

## Core framing
EmmaNeigh is best described as a **model-agnostic execution layer for legal workflows**.

That unifies the two product ideas into one:
- the **AI layer** handles reasoning, translation, and routing
- the **EmmaNeigh layer** turns that intent into machine-readable commands
- connected systems and deterministic workflows **execute the work**

This is why the product can be presented as:
- a systems integrator for legal work
- an execution fabric for legal workflows
- a USB-C hub for legal systems

## Critical product point
The execution itself does **not** require AI.

AI is used to:
- interpret the prompt
- choose the workflow
- fill in the parameters

What does not require AI is the work underneath:
- scanning Outlook activity
- updating checklist comments
- generating punchlists
- running redlines
- creating sig packets
- rebuilding executed versions
- moving files across connected systems

That is the strategic distinction: EmmaNeigh is not a browser-click bot. It is an orchestration and connector layer.

## Slide 1
### The model can reason. EmmaNeigh makes it execute.

**Message:**
EmmaNeigh sits between the model and the legal operating stack.

**What to say:**
This is not another standalone model. It is the execution layer that makes the model operational across the software lawyers already use.

---

## Slide 2
### The bottleneck is execution across the desktop stack

**Message:**
Matter administration still requires people to open files, switch systems, and complete deterministic steps one application at a time.

**Examples:**
- find the latest draft in Outlook
- run the redline in Litera
- save the clean version to the DMS
- create the sig packet
- rebuild executed versions

**What to say:**
Cloud AI can reason, but it generally stops at the system boundary. The work still lives across desktop applications, local files, and firm systems.

---

## Slide 3
### EmmaNeigh unifies the AI layer and the systems-integration layer

**Message:**
EmmaNeigh is the layer that plugs models into the actual legal operating stack.

**Framing:**
- AI = reasoning and translation
- EmmaNeigh = execution layer
- connectors = deterministic system actions

**What to say:**
Think of EmmaNeigh as USB-C for legal work. One hub that lets different models operate across Outlook, Word, Litera, iManage, DocuSign, and local files.

---

## Slide 4
### What EmmaNeigh already executes

**Current workflow surface:**
- Outlook search and attachment handling
- checklist updates from matter activity
- punchlist generation
- Litera redlines
- sig packets and packet shells
- executed versions from signed pages
- document management and file operations

**What to say:**
This is already a workflow product. The prompt layer is simply the way users access the workflows more naturally.

---

## Slide 5
### Why firms care

**Message:**
This is matter administration and paralegal work that often leaks into lawyer time.

**Key framing:**
- necessary operational work
- not high-end legal judgment
- difficult to bill cleanly
- often discounted or written off by partners

**What to say:**
EmmaNeigh is not replacing legal judgment. It is compressing the execution time around documents, signatures, checklists, and coordination work that clients resist paying for.

---

## Slide 6
### Why this is interesting for Kirkland AI

**Message:**
EmmaNeigh gives Kirkland a way to extend the model conversation into actual execution.

**What to say:**
If Kirkland is evaluating Harvey or another reasoning system, EmmaNeigh is the layer that can make that model operational across the existing desktop and DMS stack without requiring a rip-and-replace.

---

## Slide 7
### Two asks would materially improve the product in a Kirkland environment

**Ask 1: Harvey bearer/authentication token**
This would let EmmaNeigh route work through a stronger legal reasoning layer than a free foundation model.

**Ask 2: Unrestricted iManage API surface**
This would unlock better browse, save, version, and cross-version workflows inside the DMS.

**What to say:**
These two inputs are the biggest leverage points if the goal is to evaluate EmmaNeigh as a real operating layer rather than just a demo.

---

## Slide 8
### A sensible next step is a narrow pilot

**Pilot idea:**
- connect the model layer and target integrations
- validate a small set of real transaction workflows
- run a small user group on matter-administration tasks
- measure workflow speed, reliability, and write-off pressure reduction

**Closing line:**
EmmaNeigh is most compelling not as another AI app, but as the execution layer that lets the firm’s preferred AI actually operate.
