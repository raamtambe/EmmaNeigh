# Kirkland AI Pitch Deck (Boardroom Version)

## Slide 1: The Model Can Reason. EmmaNeigh Makes It Execute.
- EmmaNeigh is a model-agnostic execution layer for matter administration, paralegal workflows, and document operations.
- The core point is simple: the model interprets the request, but EmmaNeigh is the layer that actually routes and executes the work across the legal desktop stack.
- Adobe is a first-class part of that operating stack alongside Outlook, Word, DocuSign, Litera, and iManage.

## Slide 2: The Bottleneck Is Execution Across the Desktop Stack.
- Lawyers and paralegals still have to move between Outlook, Litera, iManage, Adobe, DocuSign, Word, and local files to finish deterministic workflow steps.
- The Harvey example makes the problem concrete: the model can understand the request, but it cannot directly manipulate files or operate the desktop environment.
- This is the gap EmmaNeigh is designed to close.

## Slide 3: EmmaNeigh Unifies Reasoning and Execution.
- AI determines what should happen.
- EmmaNeigh determines how to execute it across software, files, and connected systems.
- Execution itself does not require AI. It requires connectors, file operations, and operating-system access.
- That is why the product can stay model agnostic while still benefiting from stronger reasoning systems over time.

## Slide 4: What EmmaNeigh Already Executes
- Checklist updates from email activity
- Punchlists from working checklists
- Litera redlines
- PDF workflows in Adobe-oriented environments
- Executed versions from signature materials
- Document movement and system routing

## Slide 5: Why Firms Care
- This is matter administration and paralegal work that often spills into lawyer time.
- It is operational rather than legal-judgment-heavy.
- When lawyers do it anyway, it is often difficult to bill fully and easy to write off.
- The value proposition is reduced operational drag and lower partner write-off pressure.

## Slide 6: Why This Is Interesting for Kirkland AI
- EmmaNeigh is not another model discussion.
- It is the execution surface that can sit beneath Harvey or another preferred reasoning layer.
- If Kirkland wants to move from AI analysis into real execution across its existing systems, this is the layer that makes that possible.

## Slide 7: Two Inputs Would Materially Improve the Product at Kirkland
- Harvey bearer / authentication token
  - This would let EmmaNeigh plug into a stronger legal reasoning system while keeping the execution architecture model agnostic.
- Unrestricted iManage API surface
  - This would unlock fuller browse, save, version-aware, and compare workflows inside the DMS.

## Slide 8: A Sensible Next Step Is a Narrow Pilot
- The right next step is a narrow pilot, not a broad rollout.
- Focus on a small number of transaction workflows with the required permissions and integrations.
- Success metrics:
  - faster checklist maintenance
  - less manual packet work
  - lower write-off pressure
  - a clearer integration roadmap

## Tone Guidance
- This version is intended for Kirkland partners, innovation leadership, and boardroom-style discussions.
- The visual and narrative treatment is intentionally closer to a McKinsey-style client discussion: flatter design, tighter framing, clearer operating logic, and less startup-style energy.
- The emphasis is on operational leverage, system integration, and the ability to sit beneath the firm’s preferred reasoning layer rather than on founder-style product excitement.
